## Executive Summary

This warning advises Guild branches that Imperial seizure authority may be asserted around the Jekara auction event if Imperial representatives classify the carbonite prisoner as Rebel intelligence property or Imperial bait. The warning is procedural and jurisdictional; it does not give hunters authority to interfere with Imperial operations.

The record reflects confirmed Imperial interest and branch-level risk, not confirmed final custody. Darth Vader's reported intervention creates a severe operational constraint for hunters, but the Guild archive must not treat Imperial intent as completed transfer unless a custody record confirms it.

## Background

The Empire's interest in Han Solo is tied to Rebel leverage, intelligence value, and the aftermath of the Hoth evacuation. Reports from the auction window indicate high-level Imperial involvement after syndicate bidding drew multiple powers to Jekara.

Guild liaison staff are warning hunters that a valid client contract does not override an Imperial seizure claim. Jabba's court, Crimson Dawn, and other syndicate parties may assert property rights, but Imperial military authority can supersede local or underworld arrangements at inspected ports, transit lanes, and seized vessels.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Imperial interest in the carbonite prisoner is confirmed in Guild liaison traffic.
- High-level Imperial intervention is reported in the Jekara auction window.
- Darth Vader is named in Imperial-linked auction reports reviewed by the liaison desk.
- Guild contracts do not override Imperial seizure authority.
- Hunters carrying auction-linked custody tokens, fobs, or manifests may face inspection.
- No Guild record attached to this warning confirms final Imperial custody of the carbonite prisoner.
- Branches are instructed to route all Imperial communications through liaison channels.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One Imperial clerk states the prisoner is "already Imperial property"; no custody receipt accompanies the statement.
- Hutt agents claim Jabba's bid closed before Imperial arrival; this warning does not adjudicate auction outcome.
- Crimson Dawn-linked brokers claim Vader's intervention was expected; the Guild has no planning record.
- Rebel-adjacent sources claim the Empire intends to use the prisoner to lure Luke Skywalker; the motive is plausible but not established by this warning.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should treat Imperial involvement as a jurisdiction hazard, not an invitation to act. Bondsmen should freeze payout claims that depend on defying Imperial seizure authority. Analysts must avoid claiming that Vader's intervention proves present Imperial custody unless a record specifically confirms transfer.
