## Executive Summary

This report assesses price movement and rumor manipulation involving Black Sun, Pyke, Crimson Dawn, and Hutt brokers during the Jekara auction window. The Guild confirms market movement and forged artifacts, but it does not confirm a single controlling syndicate.

The report should be used to brief hunters on inflated lead prices, duplicated rumors, and route premiums. It should not be used to accuse any syndicate of controlling the auction outcome without direct evidence.

## Background

The Jekara auction has created a temporary premium on anything that sounds connected to carbonite custody, Rebel leverage, or syndicate access. Black Sun courier claims, Pyke route surcharges, Hutt bid assertions, and Crimson Dawn invitation fragments are circulating in the same broker channels.

Market movement is real even where the underlying claims are false. Hunters are paying more for leads; fuel and docking brokers are charging risk fees; and clients are attempting to recast ordinary recovery work as auction-adjacent.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Nar Shaddaa brokers increased prices for Jekara-related leads after the auction reports.
- Pyke-adjacent Kessel route fees rose during the same window.
- Forged auction puck slugs include Black Sun and Hutt-style markings.
- At least one failed capture attempt relied on a false Black Sun courier lead.
- Crimson Dawn's auction activity is the shared market reference point in the broker chatter.
- No verified record identifies Black Sun, the Pykes, Hutts, or Crimson Dawn as the sole controller of the price movement.
- Hunters have submitted auction-adjacent claims for unrelated contracts.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A Black Sun broker says Pyke carriers are exploiting Jekara panic to raise Kessel fees.
- A Pyke factor says Hutt bidders are spreading false Black Sun courier stories.
- Hutt contacts claim Crimson Dawn benefits from every forged invitation, whether or not it produced them.
- One broker claims Imperial arrival ended the premium; route ledgers still show elevated fees afterward.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should expect inflated lead prices and should preserve receipts for arbitration. Analysts should distinguish confirmed market movement from unverified syndicate blame. Bondsmen should question sudden "auction premium" surcharges on ordinary bounties.
