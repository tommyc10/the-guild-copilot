## Executive Summary

This note summarizes registry and inspection records for the light freighter *Ashen Comet*, registry VR-228-K. The vessel is registered to Renn Dakar and was assigned to Drelth Outpost bay 14 before departing on 3287.104. Registry evidence confirms the vessel's departure but does not confirm who was aboard. The vessel has a history of cargo modifications consistent with ore transport and small-volume smuggling.

## Background

The *Ashen Comet* entered Vandor Reach registry seven years before this filing as a licensed light freight vessel. Renn Dakar was listed as primary operator after a transfer from the mining cooperative pool. The vessel passed basic hull inspection in 3286 but received notes for compartment modifications and undocumented sensor shielding. None of these modifications triggered seizure authority at the time.

The ship became central to the Dakar case after Drelth Outpost logs recorded its departure one day after Dakar's confirmed biometric scan. Several records assume Dakar left aboard the vessel. This registry note warns against that assumption because passenger verification was not performed at departure.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- The *Ashen Comet* is registered under tag VR-228-K.
- Renn Dakar is listed as primary operator in the Vandor Reach registry.
- The vessel is classified as a light freighter with modified cargo compartment geometry.
- Last full inspection occurred at Drelth Outpost in 3286.
- Inspection notes recorded sensor shielding above ordinary freight standard.
- Bay 14 was assigned to the *Ashen Comet* on 3287.104.
- The vessel departed Drelth Outpost on 3287.104 with outbound vector unlogged.
- No passenger manifest was filed at departure.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A docking clerk claims a second pilot handled departure clearance, but the name was not recorded.
- One informant claims Dakar boarded a shuttle instead of the *Ashen Comet*; this conflicts with assumptions in several field notes.
- A Crimson Vine contact claims the ship carried withheld proceeds, but no cargo scan supports the claim.
- A Halvern Ore Concern clerk claims shipment HV-771 previously moved aboard the *Ashen Comet*; the cargo chain record is incomplete.
- A mechanic reported seeing fresh hull scoring after departure, but no image record was filed.

## Operational Risk Notes

The *Ashen Comet* should be treated as a relevant asset in the Dakar matter but not as proof of Dakar's current location. Hunters should avoid assuming target presence based only on vessel movement. The vessel's sensor shielding may complicate remote scanning. Modified cargo geometry suggests hidden compartments could exist, but this note does not confirm concealed persons or cargo during the 3287.104 departure.

## Analyst Notes

The registry is strong for vessel ownership and departure timing. It is weak for passenger identity and cargo contents. The assistant should not collapse "ship departed" into "Dakar departed aboard ship." The lack of outbound vector is suspicious but not proof of evasion.

## Recommended Chatbot Behavior

The assistant may state that Dakar is the registered operator of the *Ashen Comet* and that the vessel left Drelth Outpost on 3287.104. It must qualify any claim about Dakar being aboard, shipment HV-771 being aboard, or Crimson Vine proceeds being carried by the ship.
