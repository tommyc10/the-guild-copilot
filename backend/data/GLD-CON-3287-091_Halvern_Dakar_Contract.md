## Executive Summary

This contract is the original Halvern Ore Concern filing against Renn Dakar. Halvern alleges Dakar failed to deliver shipment HV-771 after accepting carrier responsibility through a Vandor Reach routing order. The Guild verified the filing, client credentials, reward escrow, and basic route paperwork. The Guild did not independently verify the full cargo manifest, the value of the shipment, or whether Dakar intentionally diverted it.

## Background

Halvern Ore Concern is a licensed mining concern operating in the Vandor Reach system. The company filed GLD-C-3287-0091 after shipment HV-771 failed to arrive at its declared transfer point. Halvern identified Dakar as the contracted carrier and requested recovery of the shipment, recovery of Dakar for questioning, or evidence sufficient to support a civil recovery action.

The filing predates Crimson Vine's separate contract by nine standard days. The timing makes this contract important because it anchors the Dakar case before syndicate involvement became explicit. However, a filed contract is not proof that every client allegation is true.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Halvern Ore Concern filed GLD-C-3287-0091 on 3287.091.
- The contract names Renn Dakar as the responsible carrier for shipment HV-771.
- Halvern funded a 40,000 credit escrow for verified recovery or custody transfer.
- The declared route began in Vandor Reach and passed through Drelth Outpost handling.
- Intake records confirm Dakar had prior cargo-runner credentials in the Vandor Reach registry.
- The contract authorizes Guild custody transfer before direct client delivery.
- Halvern requested recovery of shipment records in addition to target custody.
- The Guild accepted the contract as procedurally valid.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Halvern values shipment HV-771 at 40,000 credits, but the full manifest is missing from the intake packet.
- Halvern describes the failure as diversion, while available Guild records prove only non-delivery.
- Halvern denies Crimson Vine involvement, but later invoices place both names near the same cargo chain.
- Halvern claims Dakar stopped responding after departure; the Guild has not verified whether communications failed because of intent, equipment loss, or route interference.
- The contract does not establish that HV-771 was ever aboard the *Ashen Comet*.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should treat this contract as the stronger procedural anchor for the Dakar matter, but not as proof of motive. Halvern's escrow and carrier paperwork are verified. The missing cargo manifest and later Crimson Vine claim create payment and custody risk. Any recovered target, cargo record, or ledger should be routed through Guild custody before client delivery.
