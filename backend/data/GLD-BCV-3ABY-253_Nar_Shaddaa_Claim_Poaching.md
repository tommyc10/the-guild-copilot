## Executive Summary

This report reviews a claim-poaching dispute between licensed hunter Mako Trill and probationary hunter Drenn Vox. Trill alleges Vox copied a bounty puck from a cantina terminal and interfered with an accepted acquisition claim.

The report confirms a dispute and a suspicious puck-transfer sequence. It does not yet prove intentional poaching or determine payout eligibility.

## Background

Guild Code discourages hunters from stealing another hunter's accepted claim, but proving poaching requires more than proximity to the same target. The Nar Shaddaa Branch received this complaint during a period of heightened concern over copied pucks and terminal exports.

Trill claims he accepted a debtor transfer contract before Vox appeared at the same docking bay with matching puck data. Vox claims he viewed a public posting and believed no hunter had accepted it. The equipment review found mismatched timestamps that require further analysis.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Mako Trill and Drenn Vox both hold Guild licenses.
- Trill filed a claim-poaching complaint against Vox.
- Both hunters presented puck data tied to the same debtor transfer contract.
- Trill's acceptance timestamp appears earlier than Vox's puck export timestamp.
- Vox's puck export record is incomplete.
- No payout has been released to either hunter.
- Code Compliance has opened a formal review.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Trill says Vox copied his accepted puck from a cantina terminal; no witness confirms the copying.
- Vox says the posting appeared public and unaccepted; terminal cache state is under review.
- A cantina clerk claims a third party accessed the terminal between both hunters; identity is unknown.
- The client says either hunter may deliver the debtor; Guild Code review may restrict payout despite client flexibility.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should confirm acceptance status before acting on public puck data. Clerks should avoid telling clients that delivery by "any hunter" settles Guild Code issues. Analysts must not describe Vox as guilty of poaching until review concludes.
