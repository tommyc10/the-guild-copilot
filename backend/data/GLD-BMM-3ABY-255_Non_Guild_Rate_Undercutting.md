## Executive Summary

This memo instructs Nar Shaddaa branch clerks how to respond to non-Guild hunters offering clients lower recovery rates outside Guild escrow. The memo does not ban clients from speaking to independents, but it warns that Guild payout, arbitration, and proof standards do not apply to off-Guild arrangements.

The memo matters because non-Guild rate undercutting creates confusion when independent operators later seek Guild recognition. Kavo Brek's Amber Quay-related claim is cited as an example of the problem, not as a general rule for every independent.

## Background

During periods of high-value bounty traffic, independent hunters frequently approach clients with lower fees and faster promises. Clients may accept those offers, then attempt to use Guild bondsmen when proof, delivery, or payment becomes contested.

Branch Master Orra Nym instructs clerks to preserve the distinction between Guild contracts and off-Guild bargains. The Guild can review some finder arrangements, but it is not obligated to pay non-members absent approved status.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Nar Shaddaa Branch has received client inquiries about non-Guild rate offers.
- Non-Guild arrangements do not automatically receive Guild payout protection.
- Guild arbitration requires a recognized contract, license, or approved finder arrangement.
- Kavo Brek's denied direct payout is cited as an example.
- Clerks are instructed not to quote Guild rates for off-Guild promises.
- The memo does not ban all contact with independent hunters.
- The memo does not change active bounty reward terms.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Several clients claim independents can deliver targets at half Guild rates; no performance audit supports that claim.
- Independent operators say the Guild is using escrow rules to suppress competition; the memo does not address market fairness.
- One bondsman claims undercutting is being financed by syndicate brokers; no funding records are attached.
- A hunter says Kavo Brek is recruiting other independents; this is unverified.

## Operational Risk Notes

Clerks should explain what Guild protection does and does not cover. Hunters should avoid private split promises without filing association notices. Analysts should not frame independent status as wrongdoing; the issue is recognition and payout eligibility.
