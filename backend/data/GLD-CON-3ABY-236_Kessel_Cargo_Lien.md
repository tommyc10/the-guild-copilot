## Executive Summary

This contract records a Pyke Syndicate recovery claim against Laro Peth, an independent cargo factor accused of diverting spice tithe crates and lien scrip from a Kessel route. The filing is procedurally valid but remains a client-submitted claim with limited independent evidence.

The contract matters because Pyke filings often carry real payment authority but can obscure internal syndicate discipline. The Guild accepted the listing bond while warning hunters that Pyke commercial language does not establish theft, debt default, or cargo custody by itself.

## Background

Kessel route work has become more expensive during 3 ABY because Imperial inspections, syndicate bidding, and carbonite-custody rumor traffic have all pushed brokers toward risk premiums. The Pyke Syndicate filed this claim through a Nar Shaddaa intermediary rather than directly from Oba Diah, citing commercial privacy.

The target, Laro Peth, is a former cargo factor who arranged temporary holds for spice, fuel, and lien scrip between Kessel and Nar Shaddaa. The client alleges Peth moved Pyke property aboard the freighter *Ninefold Debt* without remitting route fees. The Guild has not independently inspected that ship.
