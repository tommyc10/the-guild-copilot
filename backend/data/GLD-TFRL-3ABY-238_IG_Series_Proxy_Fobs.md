## Executive Summary

This log records the return of three proxy tracking fobs configured for IG-series droid handling rather than organic hunter assignment. The fobs are valid Guild equipment, but their transfer history is incomplete and they should not be used as standalone evidence of droid participation in any specific capture attempt.

The log matters because IG-series reputation has caused hunters and clients to exaggerate droid involvement in disputed bounties. The equipment desk confirms hardware configuration, not the identity or intent of any specific droid operator.

## Background

IG-series assassin droids and similarly configured hunter droids are known throughout the Guild for automated pursuit capability, rapid data transfer, and harsh contract execution profiles. That reputation makes their equipment valuable in rumor markets. During 3 ABY, several clients have claimed "IG involvement" to make weak leads sound more credible.

The three proxy fobs in this record moved through Tatooine, Ord Mantell, and an Anoat sector checkpoint. One was declared properly, one was returned damaged, and one arrived with its assignment seal scraped off.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Three proxy fobs configured for IG-series handling were returned to Guild equipment desks.
- One fob was declared at an Anoat sector Imperial checkpoint.
- One fob was returned through Tatooine Branch Relay with a damaged transmitter.
- One fob arrived at Ord Mantell with its assignment seal scraped off.
- Hardware review confirms proxy configuration but not the identity of the droid or owner that carried each fob.
- No returned fob in this log is tied to a completed capture payout.
- The equipment desk forwarded the scraped-seal fob for chain-of-custody review.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A Tatooine clerk claims one fob came from a droid that had worked near Jabba's palace; no delivery receipt confirms that.
- An Ord Mantell broker says the scraped-seal fob was used to shadow a Rebel courier; checkpoint logs do not confirm this.
- A hunter claims the Anoat-declared fob belonged to a famous IG unit; hardware data does not identify a specific droid.
- One client tried to attach a proxy-fob ping to a carbonite custody rumor; the equipment desk rejected the link.

## Operational Risk Notes

Analysts should describe these as IG-series proxy fobs, not proof of a named droid's involvement. Hunters should be careful with scraped-seal equipment because it may trigger Imperial inspection or payout rejection. Payout clerks should require separate custody or completion records before accepting any fob from this set as proof.
