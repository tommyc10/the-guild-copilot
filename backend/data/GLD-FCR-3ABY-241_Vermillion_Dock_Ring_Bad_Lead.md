## Executive Summary

This report documents a failed Guild-adjacent attempt by licensed hunter Ilyen Marr to act on a broker lead claiming a Black Sun courier had left the Vermillion with a carbonite custody token. The lead produced no target, no token, and no Guild-valid capture event.

The record matters because it shows how auction rumors are generating false targets for hunters. It confirms Marr pursued a paid lead near Jekara, but it does not confirm Black Sun possession of any custody token or Crimson Dawn transfer of auction property.

## Background

Ilyen Marr accepted a private intelligence lead from Rokk Vantos, a Nar Shaddaa broker with prior minor Guild access. Vantos claimed a Black Sun courier had departed the Vermillion's dock ring carrying a token linked to the auctioned carbonite prisoner. Marr filed a movement notice but did not obtain branch validation before acting.

The attempt ended at an unregistered relay shuttle outside the Jekara traffic envelope. Marr found only a spent beacon, a fake Black Sun sigil tag, and a partial fob ping later flagged by Equipment Intake as corrupted.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Ilyen Marr holds an active Guild hunter license.
- Rokk Vantos sold Marr a lead tied to alleged Vermillion dock-ring traffic.
- Marr filed a movement notice before leaving Nar Shaddaa.
- No custody transfer, live target, or recovered auction token was recorded.
- A fake Black Sun sigil tag was recovered from the relay shuttle.
- The partial fob ping tied to the lead was later flagged as corrupted.
- Marr's claim was marked failed rather than fraudulent pending further broker review.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Vantos claims the Black Sun courier changed vessels before Marr arrived; no docking log supports this.
- Marr states the fake sigil was planted to discredit her; the report cannot verify motive.
- A second broker says Crimson Dawn seeded the false lead to thin hunter traffic around the Vermillion.
- Black Sun contacts deny involvement and call the lead Hutt-manufactured noise.
- No record confirms a real custody token ever left the Vermillion.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should not pay for Jekara leads without branch validation. Payout clerks should reject any completion claim based solely on a sigil tag, broker statement, or corrupted fob ping. Analysts should use this report as an example of rumor contamination, not as evidence against Black Sun or Crimson Dawn.
