## Executive Summary

This ledger records the escrow hold placed on Amber Quay payout funds after Vela Tann submitted evidence from the Worlport failed capture attempt. The ledger confirms that credits exist in Guild escrow, but it does not authorize completion payout.

The disputed amount concerns whether Tann's recovered evidence qualifies for a partial recovery fee and whether Kavo Brek can claim any share through Tann. Because Brek is not licensed and no custody transfer occurred, completion payout remains suspended.

## Background

Amber Quay funded two active postings with a combined escrow pool and a separate evidence-recovery allowance. The pool was structured to reward live delivery of Merik or Rinn, recovery of listed property, and authenticated proof of counterfeit-puck manufacture. Tann submitted evidence after Worlport and requested a partial payout against Merik's contract.

Brek then contacted the bondsman desk and claimed the evidence originated from his restraint of Merik. The claim created a payment dispute and a license review issue because Tann had not filed Brek as an associated finder before the attempt.

- Tann claims her recovery work qualifies for the evidence-recovery allowance; arbitration has not fully accepted that claim.

- Amber Quay argues the damaged fob proves contract progress and should reduce its future escrow exposure; the Guild has not accepted that position.
- A branch clerk reports pressure from Amber Quay to release funds quickly before Imperial review begins.

## Operational Risk Notes

Payout clerks should not release funds based on this ledger alone. The ledger is a financial status record, not a guilt finding, capture confirmation, or Imperial property clearance. Any later payout must reference the arbitration ruling and fob return log.
