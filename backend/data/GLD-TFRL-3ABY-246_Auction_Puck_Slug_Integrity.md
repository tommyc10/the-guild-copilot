## Executive Summary

This log records the technical review of six "auction puck slugs" submitted by hunters and brokers claiming Vermillion access or carbonite claim priority. None of the slugs passed Guild authentication.

The review matters because fake puck slugs are being used to create the appearance of contract authority during the Jekara auction event. The slugs may be evidence of market manipulation, but they are not valid bounty pucks, custody tokens, or payout instruments.

## Background

Bounty pucks and tracking fobs are already under stress from duplicate-seed incidents and forged chain-code packets. The auction event has added a new category of false object: metal or data slugs presented as limited Crimson Dawn access keys or Hutt bid-recognition tokens.

Equipment Intake compared the six slugs against Guild issue formats, known Hutt scrip seals, Black Sun courier marks, and Crimson Dawn invitation fragments previously submitted to archive review. The slugs were internally inconsistent and showed signs of recycled broker templates.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Six auction puck slugs were submitted to Guild equipment desks.
- None passed Guild authentication.
- Three slugs used corrupted Guild bounty-puck formatting.
- Two slugs carried Hutt-style scrip markings not recognized by Guild escrow.
- One slug displayed a Black Sun courier mark matching the fake tag recovered in GLD-FCR-3ABY-241.
- No slug contains a verified custody chain for Han Solo.
- The slugs are not valid completion proof or payout instruments.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Brokers claim the slugs came from Crimson Dawn auction staff; no verified source chain supports this.
- One Hutt intermediary calls the scrip-marked slugs authentic but refuses to submit palace validation.
- A Black Sun contact claims the matching courier mark was copied from an old freight seal.
- A hunter says one slug briefly activated near a Vermillion traffic ping; the activation log is incomplete.

## Operational Risk Notes

Payout clerks should reject auction puck slugs as proof of custody or contract completion. Analysts may cite the slugs as evidence of forged market artifacts, but not as proof of Crimson Dawn, Hutt, or Black Sun authorization. Hunters should quarantine recovered slugs for equipment review rather than trading them.
