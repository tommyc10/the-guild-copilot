# Client Contract: Jabba Desilijic Tiure Recovery Claim Against Boba Fett

**Document ID:** GLD-CON-003ABY-099
**Document Type:** Client Contract
**Classification:** Guild Internal
**Reliability Rating:** Client-Submitted Claim
**Date Filed:** Standard Date 3.5 ABY (War of the Bounty Hunters Era)
**Filed By:** Guild Contract Intake Desk, Outer Rim Sector
**Primary Entities:** Jabba Desilijic Tiure, Boba Fett, Nar Shaddaa, Tatooine, Han Solo (carbonite)
**Related Contracts:** GLD-TD-003ABY-101

## Executive Summary

This is the original filing submitted by the court of Jabba the Hutt against licensed hunter Boba Fett. Jabba alleges Fett failed to deliver the carbonite-frozen smuggler Han Solo to Tatooine as contracted, and further alleges the failure was the result of deliberate sale to a third party rather than accident or theft. The Guild verified the filing, client credentials, and reward terms. The Guild did not independently verify Jabba's claim that the disappearance was intentional on Fett's part.

## Background

Jabba the Hutt is one of the Guild's longest-standing clients in the Outer Rim, with an extensive history of contracted work involving smugglers, debtors, and rival criminal figures. Fett has historically been one of Jabba's most frequently used hunters, which the Intake Desk flags as unusual context for this filing: a client rarely files against a hunter they have relied upon so consistently.

The contract was filed shortly after Jabba received word that the carbonite slab had gone missing from a Nar Shaddaa clinic while under Fett's custody. Jabba's filing frames this as betrayal. It does not include any evidence of a completed sale, buyer identity, or payment record. The Intake Desk accepted the filing as procedurally valid but notes the evidentiary gap.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Jabba Desilijic Tiure filed GLD-C-003ABY-099 against Boba Fett.
- The contract names Fett as the responsible party for failure to deliver the carbonite-frozen Han Solo to Tatooine.
- The original underlying agreement obligated Fett to deliver the cargo directly to Jabba's court.
- The contract authorizes an open bounty rather than a fixed single-target reward, reflecting the client's stated urgency.
- Guild records confirm Fett had prior, repeated contracted work for Jabba before this filing.
- The filing does not include a buyer identity, sale record, or payment trail supporting the betrayal claim.
- The Guild accepted the contract as procedurally valid pending further evidence.
- No competing client has filed a claim disputing Jabba's right to the cargo.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Jabba's filing describes the disappearance as a deliberate sale; the Nar Shaddaa clinic's own loss report does not confirm forced entry or unauthorized access consistent with theft, creating tension between the two accounts.
- It is unclear whether Jabba's office has independently investigated the clinic loss or is relying solely on secondhand reports.
- A rumor suggests Jabba's bounty may be intended to pressure Fett into faster recovery rather than reflecting genuine belief in betrayal; this is not confirmed by anything in the filing itself.
- The filing does not address who currently holds the carbonite slab, only that Fett no longer does.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should treat this contract as procedurally valid but factually thin. Jabba's standing as a client is not in question, but the betrayal narrative central to the filing is not supported by independent evidence in the Guild's possession. Any hunter recovering the carbonite slab, Han Solo, or information about the disappearance should expect competing interpretations from Jabba's court and should document chain of custody carefully before delivery.

The assistant should separate the verified contract terms from Jabba's accusation of deliberate sale.
