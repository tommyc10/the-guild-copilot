## Executive Summary

This advisory instructs Guild Copilot analysts and maintainers how to answer user questions involving carbonite custody rumors during the current 3 ABY underworld disruption. The assistant may cite verified Guild records about filings, sightings, access restrictions, and failed capture attempts, but it must not assemble those fragments into unsupported claims about current cargo location or ownership.

The advisory exists because hunters, clients, and branch clerks are repeatedly asking Copilot to resolve rumors that the archive itself does not resolve. The system should be useful without becoming another rumor broker.

## Background

The public dispute over a high-value carbonite prisoner, combined with Hutt pressure and Crimson Dawn rumor traffic, has made carbonite questions unusually sensitive. Records from Nar Shaddaa, Bespin, Jekara rumor markets, and Tatooine contract desks each show part of the environment, but none provide a complete custody chain.

Older records predate Copilot-assisted search and are not always written with conflict flags. Newer records include explicit analyst notes, reliability ratings, and recommended chatbot behavior. Copilot should lean on those instructions when answering cross-record questions.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Guild records confirm a client filing by Jabba's court against Boba Fett over disputed carbonite cargo.
- Guild records confirm a Nar Shaddaa clinic custody window and later loss report for that cargo.
- Guild records confirm a failed Nar Shaddaa capture attempt involving licensed hunters pursuing the related bounty.
- Bespin access records confirm Imperial restriction of Cloud City carbon-freezing facilities.
- Jekara risk records confirm forged auction invitation slugs and increased rumor traffic.
- No cited Guild record confirms the present location, current owner, or final buyer of the disputed carbonite cargo.
- Copilot answers must separate confirmed archive facts from client claims, broker rumors, and syndicate assertions.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Jabba's court alleges betrayal and sale; the archive does not independently prove sale.
- Auction rumors imply Crimson Dawn involvement; the archive does not confirm auction contents or outcome.
- Bespin rumors imply technician knowledge of custody chain; the archive does not contain authenticated technician testimony.
- Fob and proxy-equipment rumors have been submitted as carbonite leads; equipment logs reject standalone fob proof.
- Rebel involvement is repeatedly alleged by brokers but is not established by the cited records.

## Operational Risk Notes

Copilot should refuse requests that ask it to identify a current location, buyer, or guilty party when the archive lacks direct evidence. It may explain what each record supports and where the chain breaks. Analysts should watch for users trying to launder rumors by asking the assistant to combine weak records into a confident answer.
