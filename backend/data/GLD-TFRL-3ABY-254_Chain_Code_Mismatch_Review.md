## Executive Summary

This log records a technical mismatch between chain-code fragments in two puck exports tied to the same debtor transfer contract. The mismatch supports further review but does not by itself prove hunter misconduct.

The record is useful for distinguishing equipment integrity findings from Code conclusions. Equipment Intake can identify the mismatch; Code Compliance must decide what it means for hunter conduct.

## Background

The disputed Nar Shaddaa puck data came from an accepted debtor transfer contract. Mako Trill presented an acceptance record, while Drenn Vox presented a later export with incomplete terminal metadata. Both records point to the same client and debtor, but the chain-code fragments do not align cleanly.

Similar mismatches have appeared in duplicate-fob and auction-slug reviews. Equipment staff therefore escalated the issue to Code Compliance rather than treating either puck as clean proof.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Two puck exports tied to GLD-C-NS-884 were submitted for review.
- The exports contain chain-code fragments that do not align cleanly.
- Trill's export includes a complete acceptance timestamp.
- Vox's export lacks full terminal metadata.
- Equipment Intake cannot determine intent from the mismatch alone.
- The mismatch was forwarded to Code Compliance.
- Neither export is accepted as standalone payout proof pending review.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Trill claims the mismatch proves Vox copied and altered his puck; the equipment log does not establish alteration.
- Vox claims terminal corruption caused the mismatch; no terminal repair log confirms that.
- A clerk claims old Nar Shaddaa terminals commonly drop metadata; this is plausible but not verified here.
- The client says the debtor identity is obvious despite the mismatch; equipment rules still require clean records.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should preserve original puck exports and avoid overwriting terminal metadata. Bondsmen should not release payout when chain-code fragments conflict. Analysts should cite this log for the technical mismatch, not for intent.
