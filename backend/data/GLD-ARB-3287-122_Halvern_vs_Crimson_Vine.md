**Related Contracts:** GLD-C-3287-0091, GLD-C-3287-0114, GLD-CRP-3287-118

## Executive Summary

This ruling addresses competing client claims attached to Renn Dakar. Halvern Ore Concern filed first under GLD-C-3287-0091 for failure to deliver shipment HV-771. Crimson Vine Syndicate filed later under GLD-C-3287-0114 for withheld proceeds from an undisclosed arrangement. The Guild rules that both contracts may remain open, but delivery priority cannot be determined until target custody terms are clarified. Hunters may not collect on both contracts without separate verification of client entitlement.

## Background

Dual contracts on the same target are not prohibited, but they create payment and custody risk. Halvern's claim is commercially clear: a shipment was logged as departed and never arrived. Crimson Vine's claim is less specific but procedurally valid. Crimson Vine argues its claim has higher urgency because Dakar allegedly withheld proceeds owed through a security arrangement. Halvern argues first filing should control priority.

The Arbitration Office reviewed intake forms, cargo chain fragments, client payment history, and available outpost records. The office did not determine who legally owns the disputed shipment or whether both claims arise from the same transaction.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Halvern Ore Concern filed GLD-C-3287-0091 before Crimson Vine filed GLD-C-3287-0114.
- Both contracts name Renn Dakar as target.
- Halvern identifies shipment HV-771 as the basis of its claim.
- Crimson Vine identifies withheld proceeds but does not disclose the underlying transaction.
- Both clients have valid Guild billing credentials.
- The Guild has not verified Crimson Vine ownership of HV-771.
- The Guild has not verified whether the two contracts concern the same transaction.
- Hunters cannot claim both rewards on a single delivery without arbitration approval.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Crimson Vine asserts urgency but provides no independent proof that its proceeds claim outranks Halvern's cargo claim.
- Halvern denies any relationship with Crimson Vine, but two incomplete cargo invoices list both entities in adjacent routing fields.
- One arbitration clerk suspected Halvern omitted escort arrangements from its filing; this was not proven.
- Crimson Vine implies Dakar's default was intentional, while Halvern's documents support only non-delivery.
- Neither client provides a full cargo manifest for HV-771.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should clarify delivery authority before pursuing Dakar under either contract. A target delivered to one client may not satisfy the other contract and may trigger payment dispute. If Dakar is captured alive, Guild custody transfer is recommended before client delivery. If Dakar is only located but not detained, intelligence should be filed neutrally rather than sold to one client first.
