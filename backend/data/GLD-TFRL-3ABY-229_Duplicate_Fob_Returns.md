## Executive Summary

This log records the return and technical review of tracking fobs associated with the Amber Quay postings. The review confirms that at least two fob cores share duplicated seed data and should not be treated as independent tracking evidence.

The log is important because Amber Quay, Vela Tann, and Kavo Brek each relied on fob behavior to support competing claims. The equipment review reduces confidence in those claims without canceling the active bounty postings.

## Background

Tracking fobs are useful only when their seed data, chain-code source, and assignment record remain clean. In this cluster, Amber Quay submitted fobs for two separate targets, and Tann later recovered a damaged core at Worlport. Copilot cross-record retrieval found seed overlap between Merik and Rinn packets.

The duplication could indicate counterfeit manufacturing, careless client assembly, or deliberate market manipulation. The equipment desk cannot determine motive from hardware alone.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Fob core AQ-771-A was assigned to the Tavro Merik posting by Amber Quay submission.
- Fob core AQ-771-B was assigned to the Sella Rinn posting by Amber Quay submission.
- The recovered Worlport core contains fragments matching AQ-771-A.
- AQ-771-A and AQ-771-B share seed data beyond acceptable Guild calibration variance.
- The shared seed data makes the fobs unreliable as independent target-location evidence.
- No equipment review confirms the recovered core was in Merik's possession.
- The equipment desk forwarded the duplication finding to the bondsman desk and branch master.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Amber Quay claims the duplication proves Merik copied Rinn's fob data; hardware review cannot assign responsibility.
- Tann says the recovered fob was dropped by Merik during flight; no visual record confirms this.
- Brek claims he removed the fob from Merik before Tann arrived; no chain-of-custody receipt supports the claim.
- A Nar Shaddaa technician alleges the cores came from a batch sold through Hutt intermediaries; purchase records are unavailable.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters and analysts should not use fob ping history from this set as a standalone basis for target location, completion proof, or guilt. The fobs may still be relevant as evidence of counterfeit equipment circulation, but they require corroboration from biometric, custody, docking, or witness records.
