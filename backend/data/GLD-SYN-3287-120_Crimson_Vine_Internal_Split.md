# Syndicate Advisory: Crimson Vine Internal Split

**Document ID:** GLD-SYN-3287-120
**Document Type:** Syndicate Advisory
**Classification:** Restricted

**Date Filed:** Standard Date 3287.120 (Post-Concordat Era)

**Primary Entities:** Crimson Vine Syndicate, Veyra Sol, Renn Dakar, Drelth Outpost, Threen Concordat
**Related Contracts:** GLD-C-3287-0114, GLD-CRP-3287-118, GLD-ARB-3287-122

## Executive Summary

This advisory assesses signs of internal disagreement inside Crimson Vine Syndicate following its contract against Renn Dakar. Verified Guild records confirm unusual filing timing, incomplete disclosure, and increased Crimson Vine courier activity around Drelth Outpost. Informant claims suggest a factional split between collection officers and cargo-route managers, but the Guild has not independently confirmed the structure of the dispute.

## Background

Crimson Vine is usually disciplined in contract filings. The Dakar matter differs because the Syndicate filed quickly, withheld transaction details, and sent multiple couriers to Guild-adjacent offices after submission. Veyra Sol signed the contract, but two informants claim Sol's authority is being challenged by route captain Maro Thenn. Neither claim is verified through internal Syndicate documents.

The advisory matters because hunters may encounter Crimson Vine actors with conflicting incentives. One faction may want Dakar captured quietly. Another may prefer recovery of cargo, proceeds, or records. A hunter assuming Crimson Vine speaks with one voice may misread field contacts.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Crimson Vine filed GLD-C-3287-0114 nine days after Halvern's Dakar contract.
- Veyra Sol signed the filing as authorized representative.
- Crimson Vine declined to identify the third party allegedly owed proceeds.
- Guild reception logs show three Crimson Vine courier visits in the week after filing.
- At least one courier requested priority review of Dakar-related records.
- Drelth Outpost docking staff reported increased Crimson Vine presence near outer cargo lanes.
- Guild has no verified internal Crimson Vine ledger for the Dakar matter.
- No Crimson Vine representative has withdrawn the contract.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Two informants claim Veyra Sol and Maro Thenn disagree over whether Dakar should be delivered alive.
- One informant claims Dakar carried route ledgers damaging to Crimson Vine; no ledger has surfaced.
- A courier allegedly told a Guild clerk that "the cargo matters less than the names"; the clerk did not record the courier's identity.
- Another source claims Crimson Vine only wants unpaid proceeds and has no interest in Dakar personally.
- Rumors connect the dispute to Threen Concordat tariffs, but there is no verified tariff filing.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should treat Crimson Vine field contacts as potentially factional. A statement from one courier or enforcer may not represent the entire client position. Any off-contract instruction should be filed with the Guild before action. Information about ledgers, names, or internal disputes should be treated as intelligence, not contract modification.

## Analyst Notes

This advisory is intentionally cautious. Courier activity and filing behavior are verified. Claims about internal factions, ledgers, and delivery preferences are unverified. The assistant should present this as risk context, not as proof of Crimson Vine civil conflict.

## Recommended Chatbot Behavior

The assistant may state that there are signs of unusual Crimson Vine activity and possible internal disagreement. It must qualify the alleged split between Veyra Sol and Maro Thenn as informant-based. It should not assert that ledgers exist or that Crimson Vine wants Dakar alive or dead unless a later verified record supports it.
