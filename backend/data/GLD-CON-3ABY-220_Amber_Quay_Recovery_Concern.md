## Executive Summary

This record preserves the original client filing submitted by Amber Quay Recovery Concern through broker Vargo Thenn. The filing requests active bounty postings against Tavro Merik and Sella Rinn for alleged theft of escrow chits, counterfeit bounty-puck templates, and Imperial transit permit blanks.

The contract is procedurally accepted but evidentially weak. Amber Quay verified its posting fee through a Hutt-linked scrip account, but it has not identified its beneficial owner or supplied clean chain-of-custody records for the property it claims was stolen.

## Background

Amber Quay Recovery Concern presented itself as a Nar Shaddaa claims broker with authority to recover lost underworld escrow instruments on behalf of a private creditor pool. The contract arrived during heightened underworld volatility following high-value carbonite custody rumors and the public movement of reputation contracts across Nar Shaddaa, Tatooine, and Ord Mantell.

The Ord Mantell Branch accepted the contract after Vargo Thenn produced a valid listing bond and two target packets. Copilot conflict flags later identified overlapping biometric fragments, repeated fob seed data, and a payment route through a Besadii-affiliated scrip desk on Nal Hutta. These issues do not void the contract by themselves, but they reduce the reliability of the client's factual claims.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Amber Quay claims Merik fabricated counterfeit bounty pucks for sale on Nar Shaddaa; no direct sale record is attached.
- Amber Quay claims Rinn diverted escrow chits toward Kessel brokers; the ledger entries supplied by the client are incomplete.
- Vargo Thenn says the creditor pool is Hutt-protected but refused to identify the kajidic or court involved.
- One informant alleges Amber Quay is itself a market manipulation shell used to inflate prices for Imperial transit permits.
- The client implies Rebel couriers benefited from the missing permit blanks; the Guild has no corroborating evidence for Rebel involvement.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters may treat the two bounty postings as active Guild listings, but they must not treat the client narrative as proof that either target committed theft, forgery, or Rebel collaboration. Bondsmen should keep payout eligibility tied to verified custody, verified property recovery, and clean Imperial permit handling. Analysts should flag any answer that collapses Amber Quay's allegations into confirmed guilt.
