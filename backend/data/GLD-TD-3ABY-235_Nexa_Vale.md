## Executive Summary

This dossier compiles current Guild intelligence on Nexa Vale, a Coruscant-based slicer suspected by clients and port authorities of selling altered chain-code packet headers to bounty brokers. Vale is not confirmed as a Rebel operative, Imperial asset, or syndicate employee.

The dossier matters because corrupted chain-code headers can contaminate bounty pucks, tracking fobs, and proof-of-completion records across multiple branches. Vale's last confirmed sighting is in Coruscant's lower transit levels, not on Corellia or Nar Shaddaa despite broker rumors placing her there.

## Background

Nexa Vale first appears in Guild records as a contract data cleaner hired by a Coruscant bondsman cooperative to reconcile old payout ledgers. Her technical skill made her useful to brokers who wanted contract packets formatted for fast branch intake. That legitimate work blurred into suspicion after duplicated packet headers appeared in three unrelated bounty filings.

Imperial Security Bureau interest is possible but not confirmed. An ISB clerk requested copies of two corrupted packet samples, but the request did not include a public warrant or a statement of charges against Vale.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Nexa Vale performed short-term data reconciliation work for a Coruscant bondsman cooperative.
- Three unrelated Guild filings contained altered packet headers using a formatting pattern associated with Vale's prior work.
- Vale's last confirmed biometric match was logged at Coruscant lower transit level Cresh-19 on Standard Date 3.7.23 ABY.
- An Imperial Security Bureau clerk requested copies of two corrupted packet samples.
- No public Imperial warrant naming Vale is attached to the Guild file.
- No Guild record confirms Vale's presence on Corellia, Nar Shaddaa, Kessel, or Tatooine after the Coruscant match.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A Corellian dock broker claims Vale sells clean headers to hunters seeking faster customs passage; no payment record is attached.
- A Nar Shaddaa slicer says Vale is being framed by a rival packet shop connected to Black Sun; this is unverified.
- One ISB-adjacent rumor calls Vale a Rebel document runner; the Guild has no corroborating evidence.
- A bounty broker claims Vale designed the duplicated fob seeds in the Amber Quay cluster; equipment records do not prove that connection.

## Operational Risk Notes

Analysts should treat Vale as a person of interest for packet integrity, not as a confirmed author of every corrupted fob or puck in circulation. Hunters should be careful when relying on packet headers linked to third-party brokers, especially in Imperial checkpoints. Do not infer Rebel or syndicate affiliation from technical similarity alone.
