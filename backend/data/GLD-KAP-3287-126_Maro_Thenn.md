## Executive Summary

This profile maps Maro Thenn's possible relevance to the Renn Dakar matter. Thenn is a Crimson Vine route captain associated with cargo movement along the Vandor Reach and Threen border lanes. Verified records place Thenn inside Crimson Vine logistics, but direct contact between Thenn and Dakar remains unproven. Informant claims suggest Thenn's people asked about Dakar separately from Veyra Sol's filing office.

## Background

Thenn appears in Guild files as a logistics intermediary, not as a registered Guild client. His name surfaces in route invoices, cargo security references, and two prior arbitration attachments involving Crimson Vine deliveries. He is not the authorized signatory for GLD-C-3287-0114; that role belongs to Veyra Sol. The distinction matters because several rumors imply internal Crimson Vine disagreement over whether Dakar should be retrieved, silenced, or used to recover missing records.

The Associate Mapping Cell opened this profile after the Blue Cask informant named Thenn and after the Syndicate Desk flagged possible friction inside Crimson Vine.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Maro Thenn is identified in Guild files as a Crimson Vine route captain.
- Thenn's name appears in two prior cargo security attachments involving Vandor Reach lanes.
- Thenn is not listed as the authorized signer of GLD-C-3287-0114.
- Veyra Sol signed the Dakar contract on behalf of Crimson Vine.
- Thenn's route area overlaps Drelth Outpost and the Threen Concordat border region.
- Two invoices reviewed by the Guild list Thenn's routing mark near cargo chains later associated with Halvern and Crimson Vine.
- No Guild record confirms Thenn filed a bounty claim against Dakar.
- No biometric or direct interview record places Thenn with Dakar.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A cantina informant claims Thenn's people were asking about Dakar before Orin Vale's shuttle intercept.
- One Syndicate-adjacent source claims Thenn disagreed with Veyra Sol about whether Dakar should be delivered alive; this is not verified.
- A route broker claims Thenn handled a ledger pouch connected to Dakar, but no ledger has surfaced.
- A Crimson Vine courier denied that Thenn had any role in the Dakar filing.
- It is unclear whether Thenn's interest is commercial, internal political, or unrelated route housekeeping.

## Operational Risk Notes

Thenn should be treated as a relevant associate, not a confirmed conspirator. His route authority makes him plausible as a source of pressure or misinformation, but the archive does not prove direct coordination with Dakar. Hunters and analysts should avoid using Thenn's name as proof of Crimson Vine's motive without a verified internal document.
