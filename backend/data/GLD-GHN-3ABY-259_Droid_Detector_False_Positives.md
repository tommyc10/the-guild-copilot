## Executive Summary

This notice records false positives from a droid detector gate at the Mos Espa Posting Relay. The detector flagged proxy fobs, restraining-bolt residue, and one hunter's toolkit as possible unauthorized droid ingress.

The issue is mundane but operationally useful: a detector alert does not prove an IG-series droid entered the branch, and it should not be used as evidence in bounty claims without supporting records.

## Background

Mos Espa Posting Relay installed a detector gate after increased traffic from droid-assisted hunters, IG-series proxy equipment, and palace-adjacent courier work. The gate is intended to alert clerks to unauthorized droid presence near posting terminals, not to establish contract facts.

False positives began after hunters carried returned proxy fobs and spare droid parts through the gate. Facilities Desk recalibrated the detector and instructed clerks to log alerts as facilities events unless tied to a specific evidence receipt.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Mos Espa Posting Relay detector gate produced multiple false positives.
- Returned IG-series proxy fobs triggered one alert sequence.
- Restraining-bolt residue triggered another alert.
- A hunter toolkit containing droid servomotor parts triggered a third alert.
- Facilities Desk recalibrated the detector.
- No alert in this notice confirms an unauthorized IG-series droid entered the branch.
- Detector alerts are not accepted as bounty proof without supporting records.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A clerk claims an IG unit waited outside the relay during one alert; no visual record confirms this.
- A hunter says the detector was tuned to harass droid-assisted operators; no facilities instruction supports that.
- One palace courier claims droid parts were planted to slow a Hutt-related message; this is unverified.
- A broker tried to cite the alert as proof of IG involvement in a separate contract; the desk rejected it.

## Operational Risk Notes

Clerks should treat detector alerts as facilities events unless tied to authenticated equipment intake. Analysts should not treat false positives as proof of droid involvement. Hunters should expect delays when carrying proxy fobs or droid parts through posting offices.
