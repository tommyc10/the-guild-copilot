## Executive Summary

This report records an informant interview conducted at the Blue Cask cantina on Drelth Outpost. The informant, Miree Tosk, claims Renn Dakar met with a Crimson Vine courier shortly before the bay 14 power cycle and then avoided the public concourse. The report is useful for identifying rumor paths and possible associates, but it is not verified by scan, camera, or transaction record.

## Background

The Blue Cask is a low-tier docking cantina near Drelth Outpost service corridors. Guild field contacts use the venue for informal reporting because dockhands, cargo runners, and route brokers frequently gather there after shift change. Tosk has provided two accurate tips in prior smuggling cases, but also one false ship-sighting report that was later contradicted by port authority logs.

Tosk approached a Guild runner after hearing that hunters were asking about Dakar. She requested a small information fee and named Maro Thenn as the Crimson Vine route captain whose people were "asking the same questions." No direct interview with Thenn occurred.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Miree Tosk was interviewed by a Guild field contact at the Blue Cask on 3287.115.
- Tosk previously appeared in Guild files as a paid low-level informant.
- Tosk identified Renn Dakar by name and linked the claim to Drelth Outpost bay traffic.
- Tosk named Maro Thenn as a Crimson Vine route captain.
- The Guild paid a low-confidence information fee, not a verified intelligence bonus.
- No camera still, biometric scan, payment receipt, or docking badge record accompanied the report.
- The report was filed after Jessa Vane's failed capture report and before Orin Vale's shuttle intercept filing.
- The report does not prove Dakar boarded any specific vessel.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Tosk claims Dakar met a Crimson Vine courier before the bay 14 power cycle, but the courier is not identified.
- Tosk claims Dakar avoided the public concourse after the meeting; no camera record confirms this.
- Tosk claims Maro Thenn's people were looking for Dakar separately from Veyra Sol's office; this supports a possible internal split but remains hearsay.
- Tosk says Dakar mentioned "a ledger problem," but the Guild has not received or verified any ledger.
- Tosk first said Dakar left through bay 14, then later admitted she did not see him board the *Ashen Comet*.

## Operational Risk Notes

Cantina reports are useful for leads, not proof. This report should be paired with docking records and arbitration filings before drawing conclusions. The named link to Maro Thenn matters because other records mention possible Crimson Vine disagreement, but this report cannot establish that Thenn controlled the shuttle rumor or that Dakar possessed ledgers.
