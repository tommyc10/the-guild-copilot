## Executive Summary

This notice advises Guild branches that traffic around Jekara is under temporary hold status due to Crimson Dawn auction activity aboard the Vermillion. The hold does not close Guild contracts, but it warns hunters that docking clearances, fob declarations, and client credentials are being distorted by syndicate and Imperial presence.

Operationally, this record should be used as a live routing warning. It confirms traffic pressure around Jekara and the Vermillion, not the current custody status of the carbonite cargo or the lawful owner of any auctioned asset.

## Background

Crimson Dawn's public reemergence has drawn multiple galactic powers toward Jekara, including Hutt, Black Sun, Pyke, and Imperial representatives. Guild branches are receiving rushed travel requests from hunters who believe proximity to Jekara will improve claim position on carbonite-related contracts.

The Guild has no standing office on Jekara and cannot guarantee neutral docking or safe branch support near the Vermillion. Several brokers are selling "auction lane" access codes that do not match Guild routing formats. The current instruction is to slow, verify, and avoid treating urgent broker language as Guild clearance.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Crimson Dawn auction traffic is confirmed near Jekara during the current 3 ABY event window.
- The Vermillion is the central vessel named in branch traffic reports.
- Guild branches have received increased route requests from hunters seeking Jekara access.
- Hutt, Black Sun, Pyke, and Imperial representatives are all named in separate auction-related reports.
- No Guild branch has issued blanket authorization to board the Vermillion.
- Broker-provided "auction lane" codes reviewed by the Routing Desk do not match Guild clearance formats.
- Jekara traffic reports should be treated as live-event routing data, not custody proof.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Several brokers claim Crimson Dawn is selling hunter access to the Vermillion; no Guild-verified access list exists.
- A Tatooine courier claims Jabba's agents have reserved an entire docking ring; Jekara traffic logs are not available to confirm this.
- One Pyke-affiliated fuel factor says Black Sun is blocking lanes to inflate access fees; this is unverified.
- Imperial chatter suggests high-level intervention, but this notice does not confirm which Imperial official is physically present.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should not treat Jekara proximity as proof of contract priority or payout eligibility. Branch masters should warn members that docking interference, forged clearances, and syndicate pressure are likely. Analysts must keep traffic confirmation separate from custody claims involving Han Solo, Crimson Dawn, Jabba's court, or the Empire.
