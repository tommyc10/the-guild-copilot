## Executive Summary

This warning records Imperial checkpoint requirements for hunters carrying bounty pucks, tracking fobs, restraint authorizations, or recovered chain-code media through the Anoat sector. The Empire has not banned Guild traffic, but it is requiring declaration of certain equipment at inspected ports and transfer lanes.

The warning is current for 3 ABY traffic moving near Bespin, the Ison Corridor, and Outer Rim routes used by hunters following Rebel, Hutt, or syndicate leads. It should be cited for jurisdiction risk, not as evidence that any specific hunter or target is wanted by the Empire.

## Background

Imperial enforcement in the Anoat sector intensified after the evacuation of Rebel forces from Hoth and the later disruption around Cloud City. Hunters using Ord Mantell, Nar Shaddaa, or Bespin as stepping-stones now face inconsistent inspection standards, particularly when their gear contains forged or duplicated chain-code data.

The Guild liaison desk negotiated verbal tolerance for ordinary bounty equipment, but Imperial officers are treating undeclared pucks and fobs as potential contraband when linked to customs fraud, Rebel transport, or duplicate postings.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Imperial Customs Bureau checkpoint orders require declaration of bounty pucks and tracking fobs at selected Anoat sector ports.
- The order applies most consistently near Cloud City, Ison Corridor transfer lanes, and inspected fuel stops.
- Guild licenses may reduce detention risk but do not exempt a hunter from equipment declaration.
- Equipment containing duplicated chain-code seeds may be held for technical inspection.
- The order does not name a specific Guild hunter as an Imperial fugitive.
- The order does not cancel any active Guild bounty posting.
- Ord Mantell Branch received a copy because several hunters use Ord Mantell as a staging point for Anoat traffic.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One checkpoint officer allegedly told hunters that Rebel-linked bounties receive priority clearance; the liaison desk has no written confirmation.
- Nar Shaddaa brokers claim Imperial clerks are selling private copies of declared puck lists; no verified leak has been found.
- A Bespin fuel agent says Hutt-backed hunters are waved through more often than independent operators; inspection logs are incomplete.
- Several hunters believe the rule was written after a droid-carried fob triggered a false Rebel scan; the liaison desk cannot confirm the incident.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should expect equipment inspection and possible delay when traveling through Anoat sector checkpoints. Branch staff should remind hunters that declaration does not transfer Guild custody to the Empire, but refusal to declare may result in seizure. Analysts should not treat checkpoint delay as evidence that a hunter is under Imperial warrant.
