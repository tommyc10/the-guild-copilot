## Executive Summary

This report documents the failed attempt to secure Tavro Merik at Worlport Transfer Yard on Ord Mantell. Vela Tann filed the attempt as a Guild action, while independent hunter Kavo Brek claims he had already restrained Merik and that Tann's intervention caused the target's escape.

The record confirms that a pursuit occurred and that evidence was recovered. It does not confirm Merik was ever in Guild custody, and it does not establish that either Tann or Brek completed the contract.

## Background

Worlport Transfer Yard is a high-traffic freight interchange used by licensed haulers, independent brokers, and syndicate couriers moving between the Corellian trade spine and Outer Rim contracts. The yard was already under Imperial Customs scrutiny due to suspected forged permit blanks.

Tann accepted the Merik posting after receiving a location tip from Brek. Her after-action filing states she found a damaged fob and restraint residue near a loading gantry, then pursued a fleeing subject matching Merik's description. Brek's statement reverses the order, claiming he had Merik contained until Tann challenged his claim status.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Vela Tann was present at Worlport Transfer Yard during the reported attempt.
- Kavo Brek was also present and is not a licensed Guild hunter.
- A damaged tracking fob core was recovered near loading gantry 12.
- A partial biometric scrape from the gantry is consistent with Tavro Merik but below full Guild custody threshold.
- No custody transfer, restraint receipt, or live delivery confirmation was filed.
- Local port security logged a disturbance and temporary cargo-lane closure.
- Merik remained listed as active after the incident.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Tann states she never saw Merik fully restrained; Brek says Merik was bound and then escaped during Tann's challenge.
- Brek claims a second figure wearing an Imperial loader's coat cut Merik loose; port security did not identify that person.
- One yard worker says Tann fired first; another says Brek triggered the disturbance by drawing on Tann.
- Amber Quay claims the recovered fob proves Merik handled counterfeit devices; the fob log identifies the core as unreliable.

## Operational Risk Notes

This report should not be used as completion proof. Hunters pursuing Merik should assume the Worlport incident increased target caution and may have spread awareness of Guild interest. Branch staff should preserve all witness contradictions rather than selecting a convenient narrative for payout or discipline.
