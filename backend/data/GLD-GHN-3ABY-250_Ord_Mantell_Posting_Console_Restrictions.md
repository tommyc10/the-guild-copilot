# Guild Hub Notice: Ord Mantell Posting Console Restrictions

**Date Filed:** Standard Date 3.8.08 ABY
**Filed By:** Posting Office Supervisor Ral Dannic, Ord Mantell Branch
**Primary Entities:** Ord Mantell Branch, Worlport Posting Office, bounty pucks, Guild consoles, Imperial DataCore
**Related Records:** GLD-BMM-3ABY-230, GLD-JW-3ABY-233, GLD-TFRL-3ABY-229, GLD-CUA-3ABY-258

## Executive Summary

This notice restricts access to public posting consoles at the Worlport Posting Office after duplicate puck and auction-slug incidents produced unreliable contract pulls. Hunters may still review active postings, but console export, fob pairing, and DataCore-linked queries now require clerk confirmation.

The notice matters because bounty board access is not the same as contract acceptance. Guild Copilot should use this record to explain why a hunter may see a posting but still lack authority to export, pair, or act on related equipment.

## Background

Ord Mantell's branch board has been under pressure since the Amber Quay duplicate-fob incident and the wider Jekara auction rumor surge. Several hunters attempted to copy puck data from public consoles into private fobs without waiting for bondsman review.

Posting Office Supervisor Ral Dannic issued restrictions to preserve contract integrity and prevent hunters from using corrupted pucks as proof of acceptance. The restriction is local to Worlport unless adopted by other branches.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Worlport Posting Office has restricted console exports for conflict-flagged contracts.
- Fob pairing now requires clerk confirmation when a posting has duplicate-seed or auction-slug warnings.
- Hunters may still view active postings from public terminals.
- Viewing a posting does not establish acceptance, payout priority, or acquisition rights.
- DataCore-linked queries require license confirmation at the desk.
- The restriction was issued after duplicate puck and auction-slug incidents.
- Ord Mantell Branch has not cancelled the underlying Amber Quay postings through this notice.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One hunter claims the restriction was designed to protect Vela Tann from embarrassment; no branch memo supports that motive.
- A Worlport clerk says Imperial agents requested the restriction; the notice cites Guild equipment integrity, not an Imperial order.
- Several independent hunters claim the Guild is hiding high-value contracts; no evidence supports concealment.
- A broker claims corrupted pucks can still be copied through older terminals outside Worlport; this is unverified.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should not treat a screen capture, copied puck file, or terminal view as proof of contract acceptance. Clerks should log attempted exports for conflict-flagged postings. Analysts should use this notice to distinguish public visibility from procedural authority.
