## Executive Summary

This report reviews whether probationary hunter Vela Tann violated Guild Code provisions during the Worlport failed capture attempt and subsequent payout filing. The report identifies procedural failures but does not find enough evidence for suspension at filing time.

The central issue is Tann's use of information from Kavo Brek, an independent non-Guild hunter, without filing an association notice before claiming evidence credit. The report also examines allegations that Tann overstated her role in the failed Merik attempt.

## Background

Guild Code allows licensed hunters to purchase tips and work with local informants, but claim-sharing and joint recovery work require disclosure when payout rights may be affected. This rule is especially strict for probationary hunters because the Guild relies on clean claim records to protect bondsmen and avoid public disputes.

The Amber Quay cluster had already drawn branch concern because duplicate pucks reached the active board before full conflict review. A visible fight between a probationary hunter and a non-Guild operator at Worlport increased reputational risk for the Ord Mantell Branch.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Vela Tann accepted the Merik and Rinn postings while on probationary license status.
- Tann received information from Kavo Brek before the Worlport attempt.
- Tann did not file a pre-engagement association notice naming Brek.
- Tann submitted recovered evidence and requested partial payout.
- Brek contested the claim and asserted prior restraint of Merik.
- Arbitration issued Tann a probationary conduct warning.
- Tann's license remains active under supervision.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Brek alleges Tann deliberately concealed his role; Tann denies this and calls him an informant.
- A dockhand says Tann claimed "full Guild rights" over the scene; audio quality is too poor to confirm the phrase.
- Amber Quay claims Tann's public argument damaged client confidence; the branch has not accepted damages.
- It is unclear whether Tann understood the duplicate-fob warning before the attempt or only after acceptance.

## Operational Risk Notes

Compliance staff should keep this report separate from the active bounty postings. A hunter's procedural warning does not prove the targets are innocent or guilty, and it does not cancel active contracts. Future probationary claims involving non-Guild operators should be routed to bondsman review before any payout promise is made.
