## Executive Summary

This ruling resolves the immediate payout dispute arising from the Worlport failed capture attempt. Arbitration denies completion payout, authorizes only a limited evidence-handling credit to Vela Tann, and refuses direct Guild payment to Kavo Brek.

The ruling does not decide whether Tavro Merik or Sella Rinn committed the client-alleged offenses. It decides only payout eligibility and associated license consequences under the Guild Code.

## Background

Vela Tann requested partial payout after submitting Worlport evidence. Kavo Brek challenged the claim and asserted he performed the restraint work that produced the evidence. Amber Quay argued the evidence should reduce its escrow obligation, while the bondsman desk held all completion funds pending review.

Arbitration considered the failed capture report, fob return log, Tann's probationary license file, and Brek's non-Guild status. The decisive factors were the lack of custody transfer, unreliable fob core, and absence of a pre-approved finder arrangement.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Completion payout for GLD-C-AQ-771-A is denied for the Worlport attempt.
- Tavro Merik remained active after the ruling.
- Vela Tann receives a limited evidence-handling credit, not a capture payout.
- Kavo Brek receives no direct Guild payout because he lacks license status and approved finder registration.
- Tann receives a probationary conduct warning for failing to file an association notice.
- The arbitration does not cancel the Merik or Rinn active postings.
- Amber Quay's escrow obligation remains in force pending future valid completion or cancellation.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Arbitration does not determine whether Brek physically restrained Merik.
- Arbitration does not determine whether Tann promised Brek a private payment split.
- Arbitration does not establish whether the damaged fob core came from Merik's possession.
- Arbitration does not resolve Amber Quay's beneficial ownership or syndicate ties.
