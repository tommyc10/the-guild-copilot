## Executive Summary

This warning advises Guild branches that any recovered Imperial transit permit blanks, seal matrices, or customs passcodes in the Amber Quay cluster may fall under Imperial seizure authority. Licensed bounty activity remains permitted, but handling suspected Imperial documents without notice can expose hunters and branch staff to detention or asset impoundment.

The warning is an Imperial entanglement record, not a criminal finding against either active target. The Guild has not received a public Imperial warrant naming Tavro Merik or Sella Rinn.

## Background

The Galactic Empire continues to scrutinize underworld traffic in the wake of Rebel movement through commercial routes and the broader disruption of bounty markets. Corellian spaceports, Ord Mantell transfer yards, and Nar Shaddaa docking corridors are all under uneven but increasing Imperial inspection.

Amber Quay's filing specifically references Imperial transit permit blanks. That reference triggered liaison review because counterfeit permit material can be treated as Imperial property regardless of the client claiming it. Lieutenant Sorn Velik of the Imperial Customs Bureau requested branch notification if such material is recovered.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Imperial Customs Bureau sent a formal notice to the Guild Imperial Liaison Desk on Standard Date 3.7.18 ABY.
- The notice requests notification upon recovery of permit blanks, seal matrices, or customs passcode stock connected to Amber Quay.
- No Imperial public warrant naming Tavro Merik or Sella Rinn is attached to the notice.
- Corellian port authorities may hold vessels carrying suspected permit stock pending inspection.
- Guild permits for bounty work do not override Imperial document-seizure authority.
- The Worlport recovered fob core is not itself confirmed to be Imperial property.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A Corellian clerk claims Merik had informant status in a customs fraud case; no record confirms that status.
- Amber Quay claims Rebel couriers used the missing permit blanks; the Imperial notice does not repeat that claim.
- One Nal Hutta broker says the Imperial warning was encouraged by Hutt rivals seeking to freeze Amber Quay escrow; this is unverified.
- It is unclear whether Imperial Customs is pursuing a property case, a counterfeiting case, or a broader Rebel-supply investigation.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should expect delays, inspections, or seizure requests if recovered property resembles Imperial transit material. Branch staff should route Imperial communications through the liaison desk and preserve chain-of-custody records. Do not characterize the warning as proof of Rebel involvement or as a license to ignore Guild arbitration.
