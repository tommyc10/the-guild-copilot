## Executive Summary

This dispute reviews competing bounty credit requests submitted by licensed hunters Jessa Vane and Orin Vale after separate failed attempts to locate Renn Dakar. Vane claims her Drelth Outpost pursuit produced the first actionable evidence that Dakar left through bay 14. Vale claims his Threen border intercept followed a distinct shuttle lead and should receive partial intelligence credit. The Guild finds that neither hunter established custody, confirmed Dakar's current location, or produced enough evidence for bounty payout.

## Background

Vane filed the original failed capture report after losing contact during the Drelth Outpost bay 14 power cycle. Vale later pursued an unregistered shuttle near the Threen Concordat border region using an anonymous tip that may have derived from the same outpost rumors. Both hunters requested lead-credit recognition because later analysts used their reports in the Dakar case file.

The Claims Review Desk distinguishes between useful intelligence and payable bounty completion. A failed pursuit can improve the archive without satisfying contract terms. This dispute is included because it clarifies how the Guild weighs repeated weak sightings against verified custody or biometric proof.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Jessa Vane held valid pursuit authorization under GLD-C-3287-0091 at Drelth Outpost.
- Orin Vale held valid pursuit authorization under GLD-C-3287-0114 during the Threen border intercept.
- Vane documented the bay 14 power cycle and loss of corridor visibility.
- Vale documented a pursuit of an unregistered shuttle near the Threen Concordat border region.
- Neither hunter produced custody transfer, biometric confirmation, or visual proof of Dakar after 3287.103.
- No bounty payout was issued to either hunter from this dispute.
- The Guild accepted both reports as intelligence records, not completion records.
- Lead-credit status remains non-exclusive and does not establish contract priority.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Vane argues Vale's shuttle lead came from rumors generated by her Drelth filing, but the anonymous source chain cannot be proven.
- Vale argues the shuttle was a separate Dakar trail, but his engine signature match was partial.
- Vane claims a docking worker saw Dakar near bay 14 during the blackout; no scan or image confirms this.
- Vale claims the shuttle crew changed transponder behavior after his approach; this may reflect ordinary border evasion rather than target presence.
- Neither hunter can prove that Dakar was aboard the *Ashen Comet* or the shuttle.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should not treat repeated failed pursuits as cumulative proof of target movement. Two weak sightings do not equal one verified sighting. The stronger records remain the biometric scan at Drelth Outpost and the docking authority release for the *Ashen Comet*, both of which have limits. This dispute is useful for explaining why the Guild has not paid out or closed either Dakar contract.
