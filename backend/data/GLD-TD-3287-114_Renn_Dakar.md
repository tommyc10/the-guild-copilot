## Executive Summary

This dossier compiles current Guild intelligence on Renn Dakar, a mid-tier fixer and former smuggler operating out of the Vandor Reach system, wanted under two active contracts for syndicate-linked theft and contract default. Confirmed intelligence places Dakar's last verified sighting at Drelth Outpost approximately eleven days prior to filing. Several secondary reports place him elsewhere in the same window, and these reports conflict on key details, including his current ship and travel companions. The Guild assesses Dakar as a moderate-risk target: not personally violent by record, but operating under the protection of the Crimson Vine Syndicate, which raises the risk of armed interference during any capture attempt.

## Background

Renn Dakar first appears in Guild records eight standard years ago as a cargo runner for independent mining operations on the Vandor Reach asteroid belt. He transitioned into smuggling work roughly five years ago, reportedly moving unregistered ore shipments between Drelth Outpost and the Threen Concordat trade lanes. Guild interest in Dakar began after Contract GLD-C-3287-0091 was filed by Halvern Ore Concern, alleging Dakar absconded with a contracted shipment valued at 40,000 credits. A second contract, GLD-C-3287-0114, was filed separately by the Crimson Vine Syndicate itself, alleging Dakar withheld syndicate-owed proceeds from an undisclosed transaction. The dual contracts make this case unusual: the Guild rarely receives competing claims from a victim and an alleged criminal associate on the same target, which analysts flag as a possible indicator that Dakar's dispute with the Syndicate is more serious than initially reported.

Dakar matters to the Guild less for the credit value of the contracts and more for what his case reveals about Crimson Vine's internal stability. Any hunter accepting this contract should treat Syndicate involvement as an operational factor, not a footnote.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Renn Dakar holds registered Guild contract status under GLD-C-3287-0091 (filed by Halvern Ore Concern) and GLD-C-3287-0114 (filed by Crimson Vine Syndicate).
- Dakar's last confirmed biometric scan was logged at Drelth Outpost docking authority on 3287.103, eleven days before this filing.
- Dakar's registered ship of record is the light freighter *Ashen Comet*, registry tag VR-228-K, last inspected at Drelth Outpost in 3286.
- Dakar has no record of personal violence in any Guild file; all prior incidents involving him are property or contract disputes.
- Dakar worked as a cargo runner for Vandor Reach mining operations from approximately 3279 to 3282, per Outpost labor registry records.
- The Halvern Ore Concern shipment named in GLD-C-3287-0091 was logged as departing Vandor Reach on 3287.088 and never arriving at its declared destination.
- Crimson Vine Syndicate filed its contract (GLD-C-3287-0114) nine days after the Halvern Ore Concern contract, an unusually short interval for two independent claims against the same target.
- Drelth Outpost docking logs confirm the *Ashen Comet* departed outbound on 3287.104, one day after Dakar's last confirmed sighting.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A cantina informant at Drelth Outpost claims Dakar was seen boarding a different vessel, an unregistered shuttle, rather than the *Ashen Comet*. This conflicts with the docking log entry and has not been independently corroborated.
- Unconfirmed chatter suggests Dakar may be traveling with a second individual, possibly a Syndicate enforcer, though no name or description has been verified.
- One Syndicate-submitted claim alleges Dakar diverted credits intended for a third party still under investigation; the Guild has not verified this third party's existence and treats the claim as a client-submitted assertion pending confirmation.
- A separate rumor places Dakar in the Threen Concordat border region rather than departing Drelth Outpost at all; this contradicts the confirmed docking log and is currently rated unverified.
- It is unclear whether Dakar's dispute with Crimson Vine stems from the same shipment named in the Halvern Ore Concern contract or a separate, undisclosed transaction. The Syndicate has not clarified this in its filing.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters pursuing this contract should account for the possibility of Syndicate presence near any confirmed or rumored Dakar location, given that Crimson Vine has an independent and active interest in the same target. Engagement near Syndicate-controlled docking bays or trade routes carries elevated risk of third-party interference, regardless of Dakar's own low violence profile. Analysts also note the short interval between the two contract filings as a possible sign of internal Syndicate conflict; hunters should treat any Syndicate-affiliated contact made during this pursuit with caution, as motives may not align with a straightforward capture-and-return outcome. Standard Guild protocol for dual-contract targets applies: confirm jurisdiction and delivery terms before engagement.

## Analyst Notes

The docking log and biometric scan entries are treated as strong, independently verifiable evidence and form the backbone of this dossier's confirmed timeline. The informant's claim about a different vessel should not be treated as equal in weight to the docking log; it is included for completeness, not as a correction to confirmed records. The Syndicate's own contract filing should be read as a client-submitted claim, not an independently verified account, since the Guild has not corroborated the underlying transaction it references. Any question about Dakar's current location beyond Drelth Outpost on 3287.104 cannot be answered with confidence from this record. The relationship between the two contracts is suggestive but not established; the chatbot should not assert a causal link between them.
