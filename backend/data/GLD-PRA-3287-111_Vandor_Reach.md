## Executive Summary

Vandor Reach is a fractured asteroid-belt system with heavy mining traffic, inconsistent docking controls, and overlapping private security claims. Drelth Outpost remains the primary transit hub for ore runners and independent cargo brokers. The Guild rates the system as moderate-to-high risk for contract work due to crowd density, corrupted docking records, and Crimson Vine influence around the outer cargo lanes.

## Background

Vandor Reach expanded from a mining cooperative into a transit corridor after the Threen Concordat opened tariff exemptions for low-mass ore shipments. Drelth Outpost grew quickly and never received a full security modernization. Docking control, maintenance routing, cargo inspection, and local dispute handling are split across separate offices that do not share records reliably.

The Dakar matter highlights these weaknesses. Halvern Ore Concern's missing shipment left Vandor Reach on 3287.088 but never reached its declared destination. Dakar's last confirmed biometric scan and the failed capture attempt both occurred at Drelth Outpost. Later rumors place him in the Threen border region, but those claims conflict with stronger docking and scan records.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Drelth Outpost is the largest docking hub in the Vandor Reach system.
- Halvern Ore Concern operates three registered ore depots in the Vandor Reach belt.
- Crimson Vine Syndicate maintains private security contracts along at least two outer cargo lanes.
- Drelth Outpost suffered six corridor power cycling events in the thirty days before this filing.
- Docking bay records are strongest for assigned vessels but weaker for passenger movement.
- Public corridor cameras near bays 12 through 16 were partially offline on 3287.104.
- The Threen Concordat border lane receives unregistered shuttle traffic from Vandor Reach at least twice per standard week.
- Guild incident frequency at Drelth Outpost is higher than the Sector 9 median.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Several miners claim Crimson Vine effectively controls Drelth Outpost security after dark-cycle, but local administration denies this.
- One outpost official claims docking record corruption is accidental, while a Guild technician suspects selective deletion.
- Rumors place Renn Dakar in the Threen border region after 3287.104, but no border scan has matched him.
- A cargo broker alleges Halvern Ore Concern sometimes uses Crimson Vine escorts without declaring them; invoices are incomplete.
- Independent pilots disagree over whether bay 16 can launch unregistered shuttles without central approval.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters operating in Vandor Reach should expect incomplete local records. Positive biometric evidence is stronger than informant accounts or passenger manifests. Drelth Outpost's bay corridors are crowded, maintenance-prone, and vulnerable to deliberate confusion. Private security actors may obstruct or redirect hunters without openly violating Guild authority. Contract planning should include alternate identity verification and independent extraction documentation.
