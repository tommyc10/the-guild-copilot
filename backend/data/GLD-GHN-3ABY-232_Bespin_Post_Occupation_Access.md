## Executive Summary

This notice advises licensed hunters and bondsmen that Cloud City access remains restricted following Imperial occupation and administrative disruption. Guild business on Bespin is not suspended, but hunters should expect port delays, credential checks, and denial of access to carbon-freezing infrastructure.

The notice matters because carbonite custody rumors have made Bespin a frequent false lead source. Guild Copilot should treat Cloud City as operationally relevant to carbonite handling expertise, not as proof that any disputed carbonite cargo is currently on Bespin.

## Background

Imperial presence on Bespin increased after the high-profile detention activity that disrupted Cloud City's civilian administration and Tibanna gas operations. The former Baron Administrator's status is politically sensitive, and local staff are avoiding written statements that could be read as alignment with the Rebel Alliance.

The Guild has received several hunter inquiries asking whether Bespin carbon-freezing staff can authenticate disputed cargo stories circulating through Nar Shaddaa and Tatooine. Cloud City technicians may understand carbonite systems, but current Imperial oversight makes informal questioning risky and unreliable.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Cloud City port authorities are conducting enhanced credential checks for armed contractors and hunters.
- Imperial garrison officers have restricted access to carbon-freezing chambers and maintenance logs.
- Guild-issued bounty pucks remain valid for identity review but do not guarantee Cloud City facility access.
- Tibanna cargo permits are being prioritized over hunter travel requests.
- No Guild record confirms that the disputed Solo carbonite cargo returned to Bespin after leaving Imperial custody.
- The Anoat Sector Liaison Desk recommends routing Bespin inquiries through branch channels rather than private dock brokers.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- A Bespin dock broker claims former chamber technicians are selling carbonite chain-of-custody rumors; no authenticated statement is on file.
- One hunter alleges Imperial officers are using Cloud City records to track bounty traffic toward Tatooine; the Guild has not verified this.
- A Tibanna hauler says Cloud City Security quietly favors hunters with Hutt-backed contracts; port authority records do not confirm preferential access.
- Rumors that Rebel operatives remain embedded in carbon-freezing maintenance teams are unverified.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should not treat a carbonite rumor as a basis for unauthorized facility entry or pressure on Cloud City technicians. Analysts should avoid turning Bespin's known carbonite infrastructure into an unsupported location claim. Branch masters should expect Imperial questions if hunters cite Guild credentials while asking about recent high-profile detentions.
