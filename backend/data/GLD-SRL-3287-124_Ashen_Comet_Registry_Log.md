## Executive Summary

This registry log expands the prior *Ashen Comet* note with transponder, maintenance, and inspection events surrounding the Dakar matter. The log confirms the vessel's registry tag, registered operator, bay 14 assignment, and two automated maintenance pings after departure. It does not confirm who was aboard, what cargo was carried, or whether the vessel later entered the Threen border region.

## Background

The *Ashen Comet* became central to Dakar analysis because it was his registered vessel and departed Drelth Outpost one day after his last confirmed biometric scan. A registry note summarized ownership and inspection history. This log preserves the raw vessel trail used by analysts to compare ship movement against rumors about an unregistered shuttle.

Automated ship logs are stronger than cantina reports for mechanical events. They are weaker than boarding scans for passenger identity. The lack of a passenger manifest remains the central evidentiary gap.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- The *Ashen Comet* broadcasts registry tag VR-228-K.
- Renn Dakar is listed as the registered primary operator.
- A maintenance ping was received from the vessel while docked at Drelth Outpost on 3287.101.
- Bay 14 assignment matched VR-228-K before outbound release on 3287.104.
- A post-release transponder refresh occurred after the vessel left Drelth Outpost control range.
- The refresh did not include a passenger manifest or cargo manifest.
- A later automated engine-status burst indicated ordinary sublight function.
- The registry log contains no confirmed Threen border crossing entry for VR-228-K.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One technician claims the post-release refresh was manually triggered, but the system records only an automated handshake.
- A docking clerk claims a second pilot handled departure clearance; no name was entered.
- A Crimson Vine contact claims the vessel carried withheld proceeds, but no cargo scan supports that claim.
- A Halvern clerk claims HV-771 was loaded onto the vessel, but the cargo chain remains incomplete.
- The shuttle theory conflicts with assumptions that Dakar departed aboard the *Ashen Comet*, but neither route is proven.

## Operational Risk Notes

If the user asks whether Dakar escaped on the *Ashen Comet*, the archive supports only that his registered ship departed after his last confirmed sighting. Passenger identity remains unresolved.
