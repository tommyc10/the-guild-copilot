## Executive Summary

This addendum clarifies custody and evidence handling for any future Renn Dakar recovery. The Guild reaffirms that both Halvern and Crimson Vine contracts remain open, but no direct delivery to either client should occur before Guild review. Any recovered ledgers, cargo chain fragments, route tokens, or payment records must be treated as disputed evidence rather than client property until arbitration resolves ownership.

## Background

The original arbitration ruling left both Dakar contracts active while declining to determine payment priority. Since then, new records have increased uncertainty: a cantina informant mentioned a "ledger problem," ship records failed to prove cargo contents, and associate mapping identified Maro Thenn as a possible Crimson Vine route figure. These records make evidence handling as important as target custody.

The addendum does not resolve factual ownership. It sets process rules so hunters do not accidentally strengthen one client's claim by handing over disputed materials.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- GLD-C-3287-0091 and GLD-C-3287-0114 remain open.
- Halvern filed before Crimson Vine.
- Crimson Vine has not verified ownership of shipment HV-771.
- The Guild has not verified whether Halvern and Crimson Vine claims concern the same transaction.
- Any recovered target should be transferred to Guild custody before client delivery.
- Any recovered ledger, cargo fragment, route token, or payment record should be sealed as disputed evidence.
- A hunter may not sell Dakar-related intelligence to one claimant while the other claim remains active.
- Payment priority remains unresolved.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- The archive contains informant claims about ledgers, but no ledger has been recovered or verified.
- Crimson Vine sources imply urgency, while Halvern's paperwork supports only non-delivery.
- Associate records suggest Maro Thenn may be relevant to cargo routing, but they do not prove he controls the Dakar claim.
- The *Ashen Comet* records do not prove that HV-771 or any ledger was aboard.
- No record proves Dakar is currently holding disputed evidence.

## Operational Risk Notes

This ruling is procedural and should override informal client instructions. Hunters should preserve chain of custody and report any recovered record to the Guild before disclosure. The ruling reduces payment risk, client retaliation risk, and evidence contamination. It does not authorize tactical action or expand capture authority beyond existing contracts.
