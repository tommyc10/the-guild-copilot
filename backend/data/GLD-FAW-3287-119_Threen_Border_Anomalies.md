## Executive Summary

This watchlist entry collects unverified anomaly claims near the Threen Concordat border after the Dakar failed capture and shuttle intercept. Reports include sensor distortion, pilot disorientation, and claims that a shuttle entered debris shadow too cleanly for ordinary navigation. None of the reports confirm Force-sensitive activity, ancient relic involvement, or Dakar's presence. The Guild retains the entry for pattern monitoring only.

## Background

The Guild's Anomalous Claims Cell tracks unusual field reports that may influence contract risk. Most such reports are mundane: sensor failure, smuggler countermeasures, debris interference, or frightened witness interpretation. The Threen border claims gained attention because they appeared shortly after the unregistered shuttle intercept filed by Orin Vale.

Several hunters have asked whether the Dakar case involves Force-sensitive interference. Current records do not support that conclusion. No verified witness observed telekinesis, mind influence, precognition, or known relic signatures. The strongest explanation remains sensor interference in a debris-dense border lane.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Orin Vale reported losing an unregistered shuttle in debris shadow near the Threen border.
- The Threen border lane contains metallic debris known to interfere with low-grade sensors.
- Three pilots filed disorientation complaints in the same region during the review window.
- No Guild-certified anomaly examiner inspected the location.
- No recovered artifact, relic, or Force-sensitive individual is tied to the Dakar contracts.
- No biometric scan places Dakar in the Threen border region.
- The watchlist classification is based on rumor aggregation, not verified anomaly evidence.
- The Guild has not raised the Dakar matter to confirmed anomalous status.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One pilot claimed the shuttle "moved before the nav path opened," but telemetry was not preserved.
- A dockside rumor claims Dakar hired a "quiet seer" before leaving Drelth Outpost; no name or image exists.
- A Crimson Vine courier allegedly asked about relic smugglers in the Threen lane; the courier was not identified.
- Two miners described hearing a tone over comms before sensor failure, but maintenance logs suggest ordinary interference.
- A hunter claims Dakar's nickname, "The Quiet Knife," implies Force training; analysts reject nickname-based inference.

## Operational Risk Notes

Anomaly claims should not be ignored, but they should not drive contract conclusions without verification. Hunters should prepare for sensor interference in the Threen border region, not assume supernatural involvement. Any recovered relic, unusual injury pattern, or credible witness report should be filed separately.
