## Executive Summary

This ledger records pressure from Hutt-linked representatives seeking priority recognition of Jabba's bid and related recovery claims after the Jekara auction disruption. It confirms branch-level payment pressure and escrow inquiries, but it does not release funds or decide custody.

The ledger matters because Hutt agents are attempting to convert auction language into Guild payout leverage. Bondsmen are instructed to treat bid assertions, client anger, and reputation pressure as separate from proof of completion.

## Background

Jabba's original client contract against Boba Fett remains active in the Guild archive. Auction rumors now suggest Jabba or the Hutt Council may have secured a winning bid before Imperial intervention complicated custody. Hutt representatives are using that claim to demand fast recognition of Jabba's continuing right to the carbonite prisoner.

The Tatooine Branch Relay is vulnerable to client pressure because Jabba's court is a major Outer Rim client. The bondsman desk is therefore recording all communications rather than negotiating informally through palace intermediaries.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Hutt-linked representatives contacted Tatooine Branch Relay after Jekara auction reports.
- The representatives requested priority recognition of Jabba's claim.
- No Guild payout was released in response to the request.
- The original Jabba-Fett contract remains on file.
- The ledger does not contain a custody receipt for Han Solo.
- The ledger does not confirm that Jabba currently possesses the carbonite prisoner.
- Senior Bondsman Cala Venn ordered all Hutt escrow inquiries to be logged.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- Hutt representatives claim Jabba's bid closed before Imperial intervention; no Guild auction certificate is attached.
- One palace intermediary says the Guild will lose Tatooine work if recognition is delayed; this is pressure, not evidence.
- A Nar Shaddaa broker claims the Hutt Council is divided over whether to pay twice for the same prisoner.
- Imperial seizure rumors conflict with Hutt ownership claims, and this ledger does not resolve the conflict.

## Operational Risk Notes

Bondsmen should not release funds based on Hutt prestige or bid language. Hunters should continue to document custody chain separately from client entitlement claims. Analysts should cite this ledger for Hutt pressure and escrow status, not for final ownership of Han Solo.
