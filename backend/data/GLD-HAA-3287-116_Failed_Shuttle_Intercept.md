## Executive Summary

Hunter Orin Vale reports an attempted intercept of an unregistered shuttle near the Threen Concordat border lane. Vale believed the shuttle may have carried Renn Dakar based on an anonymous tip and a partial engine signature said to match a vessel seen near Drelth Outpost bay 16. The intercept failed when the shuttle crossed into a debris shadow and dropped transponder echo. The report does not confirm Dakar was aboard.

## Background

After the Drelth Outpost failed capture, rumors circulated that Dakar used a shuttle rather than the *Ashen Comet*. Vale received a tip claiming an unregistered shuttle had departed Drelth Outpost with a passenger matching Dakar's description. Vale pursued a vessel near the Threen border but did not obtain visual confirmation or biometric evidence.

The report is useful because it documents the origin and weakness of the shuttle theory. It adds a possible route and vessel behavior, but it should not override stronger docking and biometric records.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Orin Vale held an active Guild pursuit authorization on 3287.112.
- Vale tracked an unregistered shuttle near the Threen Concordat border lane.
- The shuttle did not broadcast a valid civilian registry.
- Vale received the lead from an anonymous outpost channel.
- The shuttle entered a debris shadow and was not reacquired.
- No boarding, scan match, or custody contact occurred.
- Vale filed the report as inconclusive.
- No Guild bounty payout was made from this intercept.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- The anonymous tip claimed Dakar was aboard, but no source identity was provided.
- Vale believed the engine signature was similar to a shuttle reported near Drelth bay 16, but the signature match was partial.
- A second pilot claimed the shuttle was a decoy flown by Crimson Vine, but no proof was submitted.
- A border station clerk denied seeing any matching shuttle cross the lane.
- One rumor claims the shuttle carried route ledgers rather than Dakar; this remains unverified.

## Operational Risk Notes

This report should be used as cautionary evidence about unreliable leads after failed captures. Anonymous tips may reflect real movement, deliberate misdirection, or client manipulation. Hunters should avoid treating the Threen shuttle as a confirmed Dakar lead without additional evidence.
