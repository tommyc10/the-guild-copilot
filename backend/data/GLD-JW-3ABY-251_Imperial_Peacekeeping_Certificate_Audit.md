# Jurisdiction Warning: Imperial Peace-Keeping Certificate Audit

## Executive Summary

This warning records an Imperial audit of Peace-Keeping Certificates used by Guild hunters in inspected ports. Certificates remain valid credentials, but expired seals, copied identifiers, or mismatched chain codes may trigger detention or denial of DataCore access.

The warning should be used for jurisdiction and credential questions. It does not accuse any specific hunter of forgery and does not cancel Guild licenses.

## Background

Imperial-era Guild work often depends on a hunter's ability to show both Guild license status and an Imperial Peace-Keeping Certificate. The certificate allows legal operation in certain Imperial jurisdictions but does not eliminate local inspections or branch payout rules.

The audit began after customs officers flagged repeated certificate identifiers near Corellia and Anoat sector checkpoints. The Liaison Desk is instructing branches to verify certificate seals before hunters run DataCore-linked searches or transport acquisitions through Imperial ports.

## Confirmed Intelligence

- Imperial authorities are auditing Peace-Keeping Certificates used by Guild hunters.
- Repeated certificate identifiers were flagged near Corellia and Anoat sector checkpoints.
- Expired seals may result in inspection delays or detention.
- Guild license status does not override Imperial certificate requirements.
- DataCore-linked searches may be denied if certificate identity does not match Guild license identity.
- The audit does not cancel active Guild contracts.
- The warning does not name a specific hunter as a forger.

## Unverified Or Conflicting Reports

- One Corellian clerk claims the audit was prompted by Rebel-linked searches; no Imperial memo confirms that motive.
- A Nar Shaddaa bondsman says Black Sun brokers are selling copied certificates; the Guild has not recovered a verified batch.
- Hunters report inconsistent enforcement between Corellia and Anoat, but branch logs vary by checkpoint.
- One client claims certificate delays make live delivery impossible; no general waiver has been issued.

## Operational Risk Notes

Hunters should verify certificate seal status before entering Imperial ports. Branch staff should not advise hunters that a Guild license alone is enough for Imperial operation. Analysts should avoid treating an audit hold as proof of criminal conduct.
